Why Draw Fish? Benefits for Beginners

Fish are perfect for beginner artists because:

  • Simple shapes: Fish use basic ovals, triangles, and curves
  • Forgiving: No two fish look exactly alike in nature, so there's creative freedom
  • Quick to draw: Most fish can be completed in 5-10 minutes
  • Versatile: Can be realistic, cartoonish, or abstract
  • Relaxing: The flowing shapes are therapeutic to draw

10 Easy Fish Drawing Ideas

1. The Classic Goldfish üêü

Easy

The goldfish is the perfect first fish drawing! Its round body and flowing fins make it instantly recognizable and fun to draw.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw a circle or oval for the body
  2. Add a triangle tail on the right side
  3. Add fan-shaped fins on top and bottom
  4. Draw a circle for the eye and a small curved mouth
  5. Optional: Add scales or patterns

Pro tip: Make the tail and fins flowy and organic - don't worry about perfect shapes!

2. Simple Cartoon Fish üòä

Easy

Perfect for kids and beginners! This cartoonish style is forgiving and super cute.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw a chubby oval body
  2. Add a simple triangle tail
  3. Draw small triangle fins on sides
  4. Add a big circle eye with a highlight
  5. Draw a smiling mouth
  6. Optional: Add stripes or spots for personality

Why it works: The exaggerated features make it impossible to mess up - it'll look cute no matter what!

3. Tropical Angelfish üå∫

Easy

Want something more exotic? The angelfish's distinctive triangular shape is surprisingly easy to draw.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw a tall, compressed oval body
  2. Add large triangular dorsal (top) and ventral (bottom) fins that extend high and low
  3. Draw a small triangular tail
  4. Add an eye and mouth
  5. Optional: Add vertical stripes

Beginner tip: Angelfish look impressive but use the same basic shapes as other fish!

4. The Friendly Clownfish ü§°

Easy

Made famous by a certain animated movie! Clownfish are colorful and fun to draw.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw an oval body (slightly plump)
  2. Add rounded fins on top, bottom, and sides
  3. Draw a small rounded tail
  4. Add an eye and small mouth
  5. Draw 3 vertical white stripes across the body

Color tip: Even in black and white, the distinctive stripes make it recognizable!

5. Sleek Shark Silhouette ü¶à

Medium

Sharks are easier than you think! Focus on the streamlined shape.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw a long, torpedo-shaped body (wider in the middle, tapered at both ends)
  2. Add a triangular dorsal fin on top
  3. Draw a crescent-shaped tail
  4. Add side fins (pectoral fins)
  5. Draw an eye and gill slits
  6. Optional: Add teeth if drawing with an open mouth

Style tip: Sharks look great as simple silhouettes!

6. Cute Betta Fish (Siamese Fighting Fish) üíô

Easy

Known for their beautiful flowing fins! Great for practicing fluid shapes.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw a small oval body
  2. Add large, flowing, wavy fins (top, bottom, and tail)
  3. Make the fins look like fabric or flames
  4. Add an eye
  5. Optional: Add patterns or color variations in fins

Creative freedom: Betta fins can be as wild and flowing as you imagine!

7. Simple Tuna or Mackerel üêü

Easy

Streamlined and sporty! Perfect for practicing sleek, realistic fish shapes.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw an elongated football shape for the body
  2. Add a forked tail (like a V)
  3. Draw small triangular fins on top and bottom
  4. Add pectoral fins on the sides
  5. Draw an eye and pointed mouth
  6. Optional: Add horizontal lines along the body

Realism tip: Tuna are all about smooth, aerodynamic lines.

8. Adorable Pufferfish üê°

Easy

Round, cute, and impossible to mess up! Great for kids and beginners.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw a circle or slightly oval shape
  2. Add small triangle fins
  3. Draw a tiny tail
  4. Add big round eyes
  5. Optional: Add spikes all around the body for the "puffed up" look
  6. Optional: Draw spots or patterns

Fun variation: Draw it both normal and puffed up for a cute before/after!

9. Elegant Koi Fish üå∏

Medium

Beautiful and culturally significant! Koi are larger goldfish relatives with graceful proportions.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw a long, elegant oval body
  2. Add flowing, ribbon-like fins
  3. Draw a fan-shaped tail
  4. Add whisker-like barbels near the mouth
  5. Draw an eye
  6. Optional: Add patches of color or patterns

Cultural note: Koi symbolize perseverance and good fortune in Japanese culture!

10. Abstract Geometric Fish üìê

Easy

Modern and artistic! Perfect if you prefer geometric styles.

How to draw it:

  1. Draw the fish body using straight lines and angles instead of curves
  2. Use triangles for fins and tail
  3. Add geometric patterns inside (triangles, diamonds, lines)
  4. Use sharp angles throughout
  5. Add a circle or triangle eye

Style tip: This modern approach looks sophisticated and is very forgiving for beginners!

üí° Pro Tips for Drawing Easy Fish

Common Questions About Easy Fish Drawing

What's the easiest fish to draw for beginners?

The classic goldfish or simple cartoon fish are the easiest for beginners. They use basic shapes (oval body, triangle tail) and are very forgiving. You literally can't mess them up!

Most of these easy fish drawings take just 5-10 minutes to complete. As you practice, you'll get faster. Some artists can sketch a simple fish in under 2 minutes!

Can kids draw these fish?

Absolutely! All of these easy fish drawing ideas are perfect for kids ages 5 and up. The cartoon fish and pufferfish are especially popular with younger children.
